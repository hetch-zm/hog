Our Leadership

Rev. George Mapalo & Pastor Mwenya Mapalo

Hope of Glory Worship Center is blessed to be led by Rev. George Mapalo alongside his beloved wife, Pastor Mwenya Mapalo. Together, they shepherd our congregation with wisdom, compassion, and a heart for God's people. Their servant leadership and dedication to the Gospel inspire us to grow deeper in our faith and reach out to our community with the love of Christ.

Our Vision

At Hope of Glory Worship Center, we believe in creating a welcoming environment where everyone can encounter God's presence, grow in faith, and discover their purpose. We are a family of believers committed to worship, fellowship, and serving our community.

Whether you're seeking spiritual growth, community connection, or a place to call home, we invite you to join us on this journey of faith.

Our foundation

What We Believe

The convictions that shape our worship, our community, and our mission.

The Word of God

The Bible is the inspired, infallible Word of God — our highest authority for faith, life, and ministry.

One Triune God

We worship one God, eternally existing in three persons — Father, Son, and Holy Spirit.

Salvation by Grace

Salvation is a free gift through faith in Jesus Christ — His life, death, and resurrection — not by works.

Power of the Spirit

The Holy Spirit empowers every believer to live transformed lives, to grow in Christ, and to serve others.

Family of Believers

The Church is a family. We grow together in fellowship, prayer, teaching, and serving one another in love.

Mission to the World

We are sent — to share the love of Christ in our community and beyond, in word and in deed.

Strategic Vision 2030

Building God's Kingdom with purpose and vision

As we journey towards 2030, Hope of Glory Worship Center has set forth a bold and God-inspired strategic vision to expand our ministry, enhance our facilities, and better serve our community. Through faith, dedication, and the generous support of our members, we are committed to achieving these transformational goals.

Church Farm

Securing land for sustainable agriculture to support our ministry and community outreach programs.

Ceiling Board Installation

Improving the aesthetics and acoustics of our sanctuary for a more comfortable worship environment.

Parsonage Building

Completing the parsonage to provide proper housing for our pastoral leadership and their families.

Borehole Sinking

Sustainable water supply for our growing congregation and facilities.

Church Vehicles

Acquiring a bus for member transport and a light truck for ministry operations.

Partner With Us

These strategic goals require the commitment and partnership of our entire church family. Your prayers, support, and contributions are vital to making this vision a reality. Together, we can build a lasting legacy for generations to come.

First time?

Plan Your Visit

We'd love to welcome you. Here's everything you need to know before you arrive.

When to come

Sunday worship begins at 9:00 AM and runs to about 12:30 PM. Arrive 10–15 minutes early to settle in.

Where we are

Hope of Glory Worship Center, Natwange East, Ndeke, Kitwe. Parking is available on-site.

Bring the family

Children are welcomed warmly. Our Children's Ministry runs alongside Sunday worship.

Come as you are

No dress code. Come dressed comfortably — what matters is your heart, not your outfit.

Common questions

What should I expect at a Sunday service?

You'll find warm welcome, Spirit-led worship, prayer, a Bible-based message, and time to meet the congregation. Our gatherings are joyful, accessible, and grounded in God's Word.

I'm new — how do I get connected?

Stop by the welcome area on a Sunday, fill in our contact form below, or join one of our cell groups for closer fellowship. Our cell ministry is the best place to grow alongside others.

Are there programmes for young people and children?

Yes. We have an active Children's Ministry, a vibrant Youth Ministry, and our cell groups welcome members of all ages. Every age and stage has a place at Hope of Glory.

Can I attend mid-week services?

Absolutely — Wednesday cells (5:00 PM), Friday prayer meeting (5:00 PM), and Saturday morning devotion (6:00 AM) are all open to visitors and members alike.

How can I support the ministry?

You can give through Mobile Money, bank transfer, or card payment. Click Get Involved in our Strategic Vision 2030 section for full payment options, or reach out to us directly.
